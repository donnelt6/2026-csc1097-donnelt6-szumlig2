# Assessment Regulations 2026-2027

## Scope
These regulations apply to taught undergraduate modules delivered by the School of Computing during the 2026-2027 academic year.

## Standard submission times
- Unless a module brief states otherwise, coursework submissions are due at 16:00 on the stated date.
- Students should not assume midnight deadlines.

## Extensions
- Short extensions of up to 5 calendar days may be approved by the school office.
- Requests should be submitted before the deadline wherever possible.
- Evidence may be required for illness, bereavement, or other exceptional circumstances.

## Late penalties
- Up to 24 hours late: maximum mark capped at 50 percent
- More than 24 hours late: mark of zero
- Different penalties do not apply unless explicitly stated in the module brief.

## Reassessment
- Reassessment details are published after exam boards.
- A reassessment submission normally carries the module pass mark cap where institutional policy requires it.

## Academic integrity
- Plagiarism, collusion, fabrication, and contract cheating are treated as academic misconduct.
- Use of generative AI tools must follow the guidance in the relevant module brief.
- Students remain responsible for the accuracy and originality of submitted work.

## Feedback turnaround
- The school aims to return coursework feedback within 15 working days.
- This target excludes closure periods and public holidays.

## Contact point
- Regulation queries should be sent to computing-office@northbridge.example
